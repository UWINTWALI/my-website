Bio

I'm a Computer and Software Engineering student at the University of Rwanda, with a zeal for crafting software that revolutionizes user experience and boosts productivity. My ambition is to serve as the "Benevolent Dictator for Life" in groundbreaking projects, a title I cherish for its blend of leadership and ceremonial honor.

In my journey, I've harnessed cutting-edge technologies to forge innovative UI designs that captivate users. My expertise extends beyond traditional software development; I've ventured into the realm of IoT, employing LoRa technologies to enable seamless interaction between sensors and devices, delivering real-time data for instant processing and insightful analysis.

Projects

  • Online Ticket Booking Website: In my second year at the University of Rwanda, I tackled a real-world challenge by designing a web-based application to help passengers book tickets anytime, anywhere. This project aimed to streamline the ticket booking process, making it more accessible and convenient for users.
  • Student Marks Management Web App: I developed a web-based application to aid lecturers in managing student marks efficiently. This app allows lecturers to record marks on their portable devices, generate reports, and create transcripts on the go. This innovative solution has significantly simplified the grading process, making it easier and more efficient for lecturers.
  • Environmental Monitoring System: During my course at Fablab Rwanda, I created an IoT-based environmental monitoring system. Utilizing LoRa technology, this system fetches data from installed sensors to monitor changes in temperature, gas quality, and light levels. This project showcases my ability to leverage advanced technologies to address environmental challenges.
